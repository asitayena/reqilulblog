FAQs About Mark Normand

Who is Mark Normand?

Mark Normand is an American stand-up comedian and actor born on September 18, 1983, in New Orleans, Louisiana.

He began his stand-up career in 2006 and is known for his appearances on shows like Conan, The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon, and The Late Show with Stephen Colbert​​.

What is he known for?

He is known for his stand-up comedy performances across the United States and abroad, his comedy specials, and his appearances on various late-night TV shows.

He has also co-hosted podcasts such as Tuesdays with Stories with Joe List and We Might Be Drunk with Sam Morril.

Has he won any awards?

Yes, he won Carolines on Broadway’s March Madness competition in 2013 and was named Best Comedian of 2013 by The Village Voice.

He’s also been recognized by Esquire and Time Out New York as a significant new comedian and a linchpin of the New York City comedy scene.

What are some of his comedy specials?

His notable specials include Still Got It (2014), Don’t Be Yourself (2017), Out to Lunch (2020), and Soup to Nuts (2023).

How did he start in comedy?

He began performing stand-up at Lucy’s Retired Surfer Bar in New Orleans in 2006. His career took him across the country and eventually led to recognition and appearances in major comedy festivals and television shows.

Is he married?

Yes, he married Mae Planert in November 2022.

Where does he live?

He lives in the West Village neighborhood of New York City.

What podcasts does he host?

He co-hosts Tuesdays with Stories with Joe List and We Might be Drunk with Sam Morril. He also had a previous podcast called We’re All Friends Here with Matt Ruby.

What inspired his comedy?

From an early age, he was influenced by Groucho Marx and Woody Allen. He spent much of his youth making short films and performing in theater, which laid the foundation for his comedic career.
