Drew Carey Overview and Wiki

Early life and Education

Drew Carey‘s journey began on May 23, 1958, in the Old Brooklyn neighborhood of Cleveland, Ohio. Born to Lewis and Beulah Carey, he was the youngest of three brothers. Tragedy struck early in his life when his father passed away from a heart attack, leaving eight-year-old Drew and his family to cope with this profound loss.

Carey’s school years were spent at James Ford Rhodes High School, where he played the cornet and trumpet in the marching band, showcasing his early interest in the arts.

After graduating in 1975, he pursued higher education at Kent State University. However, his college years were tumultuous. Struggling with academic performance, Carey was expelled twice and eventually left the university without completing his degree.

Stand-Up Comedy: The Foundation of Fame

Drew Carey’s foray into comedy began in 1985, following a friend’s suggestion to explore joke writing. His natural talent quickly shone through, and by the following year, he was already the Master of Ceremonies at the Cleveland Comedy Club.

His stand-up career took a significant leap when he appeared on The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson in 1991, a performance that earned him a coveted spot on the couch next to Carson. This exposure led to appearances on HBO’s 14th Annual Young Comedians Special and Late Night with David Letterman.

The Drew Carey Show: A Sitcom Success

In 1995, Drew Carey’s career reached new heights with the launch of The Drew Carey Show on ABC. The sitcom, which revolved around a fictionalized version of himself, resonated with audiences for its relatable humor and heart.

Carey’s frustrations with censorship and his desire to incorporate off-color humor from his stand-up routines into the show became a notable aspect of his television career.

The show enjoyed high ratings initially but was eventually canceled due to declining viewership and rising production costs.

Whose Line Is It Anyway?: Improvisational Mastery

While starring in his sitcom, Carey began hosting the American version of Whose Line Is It Anyway? in 1998. This improvisational comedy show showcased his versatility and contributed significantly to his fame.

He was not just the host but also actively participated in the improv games, adding to the show’s appeal.

Transition to Game Show Hosting

In a significant career shift, Drew Carey became the host of The Price Is Right in 2007, succeeding Bob Barker. His approachable personality and respect for the show’s legacy helped him win over a new audience.

Other Notable Appearances and Ventures

Drew Carey’s career is marked by versatility, evident in his various roles and appearances. He has appeared in films, television series, music videos, and even a computer game. Notably, he had a role in the 1993 film Coneheads and the television film Geppetto in 2000.

Beyond Entertainment

Carey’s interests extend beyond the entertainment industry. He is a devoted fan of various sports teams and has shown support for the Cleveland baseball team. His passion for soccer is evident in his minority ownership of the Seattle Sounders FC, a Major League Soccer team.

Additionally, Carey has ventured into photography, often seen on the sidelines of U.S. National Team soccer games as a press photographer.

Personal Life and Advocacies

Away from the camera, Drew’s life has had its share of ups and downs. His engagements to Nicole Jaracz and Amie Harwick, though not culminating in marriage, were significant parts of his personal life. Drew is also known for his advocacy for public libraries and his political stance as a Libertarian.
