FAQs About Mari Kodama

Who is Mari Kodama?

Mari Kodama is a renowned classical pianist, known for her performances of a broad repertoire across Europe, North America, and Japan. She is particularly acclaimed for her interpretations of Beethoven’s works.

Where was she born?

She was born in Osaka, Japan, in 1967 but was raised in several countries in Europe due to her father’s work.

What is she known for in the classical music world?

She has gained international acclaim for her virtuosity and profound musicality, especially noted as a benchmark Beethoven interpreter. She is reputed for her precise and expressive performances of both orchestral and solo piano works.

What notable recordings has she released?

Among her notable projects is the complete Beethoven Sonata cycle. She has also recorded pieces by Tchaikovsky and Robert Schumann, showcasing her versatile musical skills.

Has she performed with any famous orchestras?

Yes, she has performed with several leading orchestras, including the Berlin Philharmonic, the London Philharmonic, and the Los Angeles Philharmonic, among others.

Does she collaborate with other musicians?

Yes, she frequently performs with her sister Momo Kodama, her daughter Karin Kei Nagano, and her husband, conductor Kent Nagano.

What are some highlights of her festival appearances?

She has been a regular at major music festivals such as the Salzburg Festival, the Verbier Festival, and the Mostly Mozart Festival in New York City.

What educational role does she play in the music community?

Beyond performing, she is actively involved in music education and serves as the artistic director of the Forest Hill Musical Days festival in San Francisco, a chamber music festival she co-founded with Kent Nagano.
