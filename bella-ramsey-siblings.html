Bella Ramsey career

Ramsey played Lyanna Mormont in the HBO fantasy drama television series Game of Thrones from 2016 to 2019. Ramsey’s first role with a credit is in this role. After making her television debut in “The Broken Man” (season 6, episode 7), fans and critics praised Ramsey as a standout performer for capturing the tough-minded leadership style of her role.

When she appeared in the season finale, the same response was received, and The Hollywood Reporter referred to her as “season 6’s breakout star.” Ramsey remained in the position for the seventh and eighth seasons of the show.

Ramsey played Mildred Hubble, the title character, in the 2017 television version of the books The Worst Witch, for which she was nominated for the Young Performer category at the 2019 British Academy Children’s Awards.

Her departure from the show in 2020 due to mental health difficulties was announced on Instagram, and Lydia Page was cast in her place for the fourth and final season.

With Luke Pearson, Kurt Mueller, and Stephanie Simpson, Ramsey received the 2019 BAFTA award for best “Children’s Animation” for her work as the voice of the title character in the 2018 Netflix original series Hilda.

The first episode of the series featured Ramsey’s singing debut, “The Life of Hilda,” which was followed by the release of the second season of the show on December 14, 2020.

Hilda and the Mountain King, an 80-minute television movie special that debuted on December 30th, 2021 saw her reprising the role.

Alongside fellow Game of Thrones veteran Pedro Pascal, Ramsey was chosen in February 2021 for the lead part of Ellie in the planned HBO adaptation of the 2013 video game The Last of Us.
